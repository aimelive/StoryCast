Voices from the Valley: Stories of the Rift

Chris Kimani and Nangila Wapula introduce Voices of the Earth, a documentary series shining a light on grassroots environmental champions who are protecting what remains and restoring what was lost.

Story

Across Africa, communities are doing something remarkable — they are reclaiming their relationship with the land. Climate change, environmental degradation, and modern systems have pushed many away from the ways that once sustained them. But quietly, boldly, and creatively, grassroots groups are fighting back.

"Africa doesn't need to be saved. Africa needs to be heard." — Chris Kimani & Nangila Wapula

Voices of the Earth is a documentary series created by media entrepreneur Chris Kimani and environmental planner Nangila Wapula. Through basic media training and community storytelling, the series gives local groups the tools to tell their own stories — on their own terms.

What the series does

From on-site interviews and expert insights to visual storytelling and community workshops, Voices of the Earth builds the capacity of local groups not only to conserve their environment, but to mobilise resources, forge partnerships, and create lasting impact. This is a story of people and nature — not as separate entities, but as one.
