About this documentary

In 2018, the NHS turned seventy years old. To mark the occasion, a documentary crew spent a night inside an Accident & Emergency department — one of the busiest and most pressured environments in British public life.

"You don't think about the hours. You think about the person in front of you. That's it. That's the job."

The film follows nurses, doctors, and support staff from the start of the night shift to handover at dawn. It is quiet and observational — no commentary, no dramatic reconstruction. Just the work, and the people doing it.

The NHS at 70

Founded in 1948 on the principle that healthcare should be free at the point of need, the NHS remains one of the world's largest publicly funded health services. At seventy, it was treating over one million patients every 36 hours. This documentary is a small portrait of what that looks like, on one night, in one department.
