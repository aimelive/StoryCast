About this discussion

For millions of Lagos residents, power cuts are not occasional inconveniences — they are a daily reality, often lasting eight to twelve hours at a stretch. This radio discussion digs into why, and who is responsible.

"People are not just frustrated. They are running businesses on generators, paying double for electricity they are not receiving. That is not a minor issue — that is a livelihood issue."

The Ikeja Electric Distribution Company (IKEDC) serves one of the largest electricity distribution zones in Nigeria. The discussion brings together a journalist who covers the energy sector, a resident advocate, and a power infrastructure analyst to untangle the web of factors behind the outages — from transmission losses and funding gaps to billing disputes and political accountability.

What residents are saying

Callers during the original broadcast described going days without power in some areas, relying on diesel generators at significant expense, and receiving bills that did not reflect actual consumption. The discussion surfaces these testimonies alongside the official response from the distribution company.
