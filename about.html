Why we exist

Every story deserves a listener. Every listener deserves access.

StoryCast was founded on a simple idea: the most powerful stories in the world are often the least accessible. We publish audio and video documentary work — and we make every single piece available with full transcripts, captions, and keyboard-navigable players. No exceptions.

What we do

Storytelling, done differently

We work with journalists, documentary makers, and oral historians to bring their work to the widest possible audience.

🎙

Audio Documentaries

Long-form audio journalism, oral histories, and intimate conversations recorded in the field. Every piece is fully transcribed with timed cues.

📹

Video Essays

Short and long-form documentary film. Embedded players include closed captions in multiple languages, plus a full written transcript below each video.

📝

Written Transcripts

Every story on StoryCast has a full, human-edited transcript — available to read online or download as a plain text file. Timed cues let you follow along.

🌍

Global Voices

Stories from 38 countries. Where stories are recorded in languages other than English, we provide English translations in the transcript and as optional audio descriptions.

WCAG AA compliance

Accessibility commitment

We follow WCAG 2.1 Level AA guidelines throughout. Here is specifically what that means on StoryCast.

Media Accessibility

  • All audio stories have full, timed transcripts readable online and downloadable.
  • All video stories include closed captions via WebVTT <track> elements.
  • Native <audio> and <video> elements use browser-native controls, which are keyboard-accessible in all major browsers.
  • Decorative imagery uses aria-hidden="true"; informative images have descriptive alt text.

Navigation & Structure

  • A "Skip to main content" link is the first focusable element on every page.
  • Landmark regions (<header>, <nav>, <main>, <footer>, <aside>) provide screen reader navigation.
  • All navigation elements are reachable and operable by keyboard alone.
  • Visible :focus-visible states use a 2.5px amber ring with sufficient contrast on all interactive elements.

Content & Color

  • Body text (#F2EFE8 on #0D0F14) achieves 16.2:1 contrast ratio — well above the 4.5:1 AA requirement.
  • Secondary text (#8B93A8 on #0D0F14) achieves 5.8:1 contrast ratio, meeting AA for normal text.
  • Amber accent (#F5A623 on #0D0F14) achieves 8.1:1 contrast ratio for links and interactive elements.
  • prefers-reduced-motion media query disables all CSS animations and transitions for users who prefer reduced motion.

ARIA & Semantics

  • Toggle buttons use aria-expanded to communicate open/closed state to screen readers.
  • aria-current="page" identifies the active page in all navigation menus.
  • ARIA labels (aria-label, aria-labelledby) supplement semantic HTML where the visible label is insufficient.
  • Story metadata uses <dl>, <dt>, <dd> for proper semantic association.

Known limitations: The native <audio> element's controls vary in appearance across browsers and operating systems, which means visual styling of the player is limited to browser defaults. We've chosen native controls deliberately — they are better tested for accessibility than custom JavaScript players. If you encounter any accessibility barriers, please contact our accessibility team.
