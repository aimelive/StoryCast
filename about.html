Why we exist

Every story deserves a listener. Every listener deserves access.

StoryCast was founded on a simple idea: the most powerful stories in the world are often the least accessible. We publish audio and video documentary work — and we make every single piece available with full transcripts, captions, and keyboard-navigable players. No exceptions.

What we do

Storytelling, done differently

We work with journalists, documentary makers, and oral historians to bring their work to the widest possible audience.

WCAG AA compliance

Accessibility commitment

We follow WCAG 2.1 Level AA guidelines throughout. Here is specifically what that means on StoryCast.

Media Accessibility

  • Every audio story comes with a full, timed transcript you can read online or download to your device.
  • Every video story includes closed captions that can be turned on or off directly in the player.
  • All media players use standard browser controls, meaning they work with your keyboard, screen reader, and assistive technology out of the box.
  • Images that are purely decorative are hidden from screen readers so they don't create unnecessary noise.

Navigation & Structure

  • A "Skip to main content" link appears at the top of every page so keyboard users can jump past the navigation instantly.
  • Pages are divided into clearly labelled regions — header, navigation, main content, sidebar, and footer — so screen readers can jump between them easily.
  • Everything on the site — menus, buttons, players, forms — can be reached and used without a mouse.
  • When you tab to a link or button, a visible amber outline appears around it so you always know where you are on the page.

Color & Readability

  • All text on the site has been checked against WCAG contrast standards. Our main body text passes at more than three times the minimum requirement.
  • Color is never the only way we communicate something — labels, icons, and text are always used alongside color.
  • If you have set your device to reduce motion, all animations and transitions on StoryCast are automatically turned off.
  • Text is set at a readable size and line spacing throughout, and nothing prevents you from zooming in up to 200% without content breaking.

Screen Reader Support

  • Buttons that open or close things — like the transcript panel or mobile menu — announce their current state to screen readers, so you always know whether something is open or closed.
  • The active page is always identified in the navigation menu, so screen reader users know where they are in the site.
  • Where a button or link doesn't have visible text, a descriptive label is added so screen readers can explain what it does.
  • Story details like author, duration, and publish date are structured as proper definition lists so screen readers can associate each label with its value.

A note on media players: The look of the audio player varies slightly between browsers, because we use each browser's built-in player rather than building a custom one. We made this choice deliberately — built-in players have years of accessibility testing behind them and work reliably with screen readers and keyboard navigation in a way that custom players often don't. If you run into any barriers while using StoryCast, please get in touch with our accessibility team.
